Step 1

A matcha bowl, also known as a chawan in Japanese, is a traditional bowl used in Japanese tea ceremonies to prepare matcha.

Gently add 1 tsp matcha and 1 ounce of hot water to your bowl or mug. The water should be hot but not boiling so as to properly dissolve the matcha without scalding or burning the delicate powder.

Step 2

A chasen is a traditional bamboo whisk used in Japanese tea ceremonies to prepare matcha.

Use your chasen to remove clumps by whisking in a zigzag motion. This creates a "thick tea" called Koicha, which is typically enjoyed in a tea ceremony.


Step 3

Add the remaining water, plus any sweeteners or add-ins, and whisk in an M-shaped motion until it's nice frothy.

Step 4

Now heat your milk or milk alternative to your preferred temperature. You may also use ice and cold milk to create an iced matcha.

Step 5

You can also use a milk frother to create latte art!
